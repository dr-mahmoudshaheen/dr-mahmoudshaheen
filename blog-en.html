Rights of the Accused in Egyptian Law: A Comprehensive Guide

By: Dr. Mahmoud Shaheen January 15, 2025 8 min read

Egyptian law contains a comprehensive set of rights reserved for the accused in criminal cases. These rights are derived from the Egyptian Constitution and the Criminal Procedure Code, and aim to ensure justice and protection for defendants.

Fundamental Rights of the Accused

  • Presumption of Innocence: The accused is innocent until proven guilty
  • Right to Defense: The accused's right to hire a lawyer and defend themselves
  • Right to Silence: Not compelling the accused to make self-incriminating statements
  • Right to Fair Trial: Ensuring fair and speedy judicial proceedings

Rights of the Accused During Investigation

During the investigation phase, the accused enjoys specific rights including:

  • The right to know the charges against them
  • The right to have a lawyer present during interrogation
  • The right not to be coerced into confession
  • The right to appeal investigation procedures
